I Think I'm Going to Miss You for the Rest of My Life

I think I'm going to miss you for the rest of my life.

Mostly when I can't sleep, or eat,

or really every time, when I'm trying to find,
a reason this all happened‚Äî

So I recall a time when you were mine.

Like the Tuesday you told me your dad left when you were nine.

Seems like no one in your life ever learned to stay,
so was it funny when I said,

"I'm not going anywhere,"

Like I had a say.

I didn't know your love was like a bet you'd win anyway‚Äî

You were taught to leave before I even learned to pray.
So I thought, that maybe this time,
just this one time,
you would want to stay.

Six months in, you were a slow invasion, a gentle claim.
Soon it was your shoes by the door‚Äî
Your books, your coffee, even your name,

written into my routines,

like you'd always been the refrain.
Like my life before you was just a fireplace without a flame.

And god, you burned so bright, I thought we'd never dim.
Thought forever was a given‚Äî

Thought you could never just end things on a whim.

But you left when the staying got too real.
And every time after when I asked you to feel,

instead of conceal‚Äî

It's "I need space"

turning into

"I need time."

It's "We should talk"

becoming

"This isn't fine."

It's watching you disappear while you're standing right in front of me.
Now you see me,
now you don't,

now I'm free‚Äî

Except freedom feels like drowning when you taught me how to breathe.
And this just feels like winter,

when you were the only summer I could see.

The last time I saw you, you came to get your things.
You stood in my doorway like a stranger making small talk,
asked about my job,
my mother,

Why I wasn't wearing my ring.

And if I'd fixed that leaky faucet.
And I wanted to scream‚Äî

Who the fuck cares about the faucet when you're already gone?
Why are we pretending this is civil,

and not devastation with manners on?

Instead, I said,

"I'm fine, faucet is fine, yeah, everything is fucking fine."

And I helped you carry boxes to your car, like my heart wasn't on the line.

I waved goodbye like we were friends,
like this was all according to someone's plan.
Like I hadn't spent the last three hours rehearsing how to stand.

Without falling apart,
without begging you to stay,
without showing you the wreckage of every word you didn't say.

So yeah, I think I'm going to miss you for the rest of my life.
Not the you that left,
but the you that stayed.
The you that dances in my kitchen, when I have no music left to play.

The you that listened when my dreams sounded absurd.
The you that made me feel seen, without saying a single word.

So yeah, I think I'm going to miss you for the rest of my life.
I really miss the story we were writing,

before you changed the plot.
I'll always miss the ending we were supposed to get,

but somehow both forgot.

You live in a world where I'm a chapter you've closed,
while I'm still here, stuck,

on the page where you proposed.

More than the future we'd build together,

long before you disappeared.
Before you left me with the blueprints, and confirmed my every fear.

I always knew that people leave,

and I accept that good things end.
But I never thought I'd lose my lover, long before I lost my friend.

And maybe that's the price I pay,

for never learning how to stay.

Or when to leave,
or how to grieve,
or what really happened the day you got lost.
Maybe our good was never good enough, when you're scared of what it costs.

But I'm scared too‚Äî

Scared of something borrowed, and something blue.
And that the unmistakable hole in my heart, will always be shaped like you.

I showed up on a silver platter while I licked you off a knife,
so now I know it's true,
I'm going to miss you for the rest of my life.

So where's the proof that we once mattered?
See, I think it all got burned.
But maybe that's the lesson that I finally know I've learned.

Some people are meant to stay,

and some are meant to go,
and you were always leaving,

I was just too in love to know.
